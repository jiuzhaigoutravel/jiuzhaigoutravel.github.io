Jiuzhaigou Travel Hacks for Saving on Transport

Jiuzhaigou, often called the "Fairyland on Earth," is one of China’s most breathtaking natural wonders. With its turquoise lakes, cascading waterfalls, and lush forests, it’s no surprise that travelers from around the world flock here. However, getting to and around Jiuzhaigou can be tricky—and expensive—if you don’t plan ahead. Here’s your ultimate guide to saving money on transport while exploring this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Getting to Jiuzhaigou: Budget-Friendly Options

1. Flights: Timing is Everything

Jiuzhaigou Huanglong Airport (JZH) is the closest airport, but flights can be pricey, especially during peak seasons (April–October). To save:
- Book Early: Airlines often offer discounts 2–3 months in advance.
- Fly to Chengdu First: Chengdu’s Shuangliu Airport has more flight options. From there, take a bus or a cheaper domestic flight to JZH.
- Use Points or Miles: If you collect airline miles, this is a great route to cash them in.

2. Long-Distance Buses: The Cheapest Way

If you’re not in a rush, buses from Chengdu or other nearby cities are the most economical choice.
- Chengdu to Jiuzhaigou: The 8–10 hour ride costs around $20–$30. Opt for overnight buses to save on accommodation.
- Chartered Mini-Buses: If traveling in a group, splitting a private minibus can be cost-effective.

3. Train + Bus Combo

While there’s no direct train to Jiuzhaigou, you can take a high-speed train to Songpan or nearby cities, then transfer to a bus. This hybrid method balances cost and comfort.

Navigating Jiuzhaigou Valley: Smart Transport Tips

1. Shuttle Buses Inside the Park

Jiuzhaigou National Park operates eco-friendly shuttle buses, and the ticket (included in your entrance fee) is a lifesaver.
- Hop-On, Hop-Off: Buses run frequently, so plan your route to avoid backtracking.
- Avoid Peak Hours: Lines get long around 9 AM—arrive early or visit popular spots later.

2. Walking: Free and Scenic

Many of Jiuzhaigou’s best sights are within walking distance of each other. Pack comfortable shoes and explore:
- Rize Valley: A gentle walk with stunning lakes like Five Flower Lake.
- Shuzheng Valley: Less crowded, perfect for peaceful strolls.

3. Private Cars? Only for Specific Needs

Hiring a private car inside the park is expensive and unnecessary—stick to shuttles. However, if you’re traveling with elderly or disabled companions, some drivers offer accessible transport (negotiate prices beforehand).

Day Trips from Jiuzhaigou: Affordable Excursions

1. Huanglong National Park

Known for its colorful pools, Huanglong is a must-visit. To save:
- Shared Vans: Split a ride with other travelers (around $15–$20 per person).
- Public Bus: Cheaper but less flexible—check schedules in advance.

2. Songpan Ancient Town

A charming stop for history lovers.
- Local Buses: Cost under $10 from Jiuzhaigou.
- Bike Rentals: Explore the town on two wheels for a few dollars a day.

Off-Season Travel: Big Savings

Visiting Jiuzhaigou in winter (November–March) means fewer crowds and lower transport costs:
- Discounted Flights: Airlines slash prices by up to 50%.
- Cheaper Buses: Fewer tourists mean more availability and better deals.
- Hotel Shuttles: Many lodges offer free or discounted rides to the park entrance.

Apps and Tools to Save on Transport

  • Ctrip (Trip.com): Compare flight and bus prices.
  • Didi Chuxing: China’s Uber—useful for short rides outside the park.
  • Google Translate: Helps with communicating directions if you don’t speak Mandarin.

Final Pro Tips

  • Cash is King: Some rural transport options don’t accept cards.
  • Pack Snacks: Food inside the park is overpriced—save money for transport instead.
  • Ask Locals: Hotel staff often know the cheapest ways to get around.

With these hacks, you’ll spend less on transport and more on unforgettable experiences in Jiuzhaigou’s magical landscape. Happy travels!
