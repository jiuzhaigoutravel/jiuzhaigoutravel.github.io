Jiuzhaigou Travel Guide: Entrance Fee + Best Photo Spots

Nestled in the northern part of Sichuan Province, Jiuzhaigou Valley is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its breathtaking turquoise lakes, cascading waterfalls, and vibrant autumn foliage. Whether you're a nature enthusiast, a photography lover, or simply seeking serenity, Jiuzhaigou offers an otherworldly escape. This guide covers everything from entrance fees to the best photo spots to help you plan your trip.

Jiuzhaigou Entrance Fee and Practical Info

Before diving into the valley’s beauty, let’s tackle the logistics.

Ticket Prices (2024)

  • Peak Season (April 1 – November 15):
    • Adults: ¥280 (approx. $40)
    • Children (6-18 yrs)/Students: ¥140 (approx. $20)
    • Seniors (65+): Free (with valid ID)
  • Off-Season (November 16 – March 31):
    • Adults: ¥160 (approx. $23)
    • Children/Students: ¥80 (approx. $12)

Note: Tickets include access to the shuttle buses, which are essential for navigating the vast park.

Operating Hours

  • Peak Season: 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • Off-Season: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM

Pro tip: Arrive early to avoid crowds, especially during autumn (mid-October), when the foliage is at its peak.

Best Time to Visit Jiuzhaigou

Jiuzhaigou’s charm shifts with the seasons, each offering unique photographic opportunities:

Autumn (Late September – Early November)

The valley transforms into a painter’s palette with golden, red, and orange hues. The reflections on the lakes are surreal—perfect for mirror-like shots.

Winter (December – February)

Frozen waterfalls and snow-dusted forests create a serene, monochromatic wonderland. Fewer tourists mean uninterrupted shots.

Spring (March – May)

Blossoming flowers and thawing lakes bring vibrant colors. Water levels rise, making waterfalls like Nuorilang more dramatic.

Summer (June – August)

Lush greenery and cool temperatures make it ideal for hiking. However, rain can occasionally muddy the lakes.

Top Photo Spots in Jiuzhaigou

Jiuzhaigou is a photographer’s paradise. Here are the must-visit spots for iconic shots:

Five Flower Lake (Wuhua Hai)

Why Visit? This shallow lake dazzles with crystal-clear waters in shades of turquoise, yellow, and green. Fallen tree trunks crisscross the lakebed, adding texture.
Best Time: Mid-morning, when sunlight illuminates the water.

Long Lake (Chang Hai)

Why Visit? The highest and largest lake in Jiuzhaigou, surrounded by snow-capped peaks. In winter, the frozen surface creates an ethereal landscape.
Pro Tip: Use a polarizing filter to reduce glare and enhance the blue tones.

Pearl Shoal Waterfall (Zhenzhu Tan Pubu)

Why Visit? A wide, terraced waterfall that cascades like strings of pearls. The mist creates rainbows on sunny days.
Best Angle: Shoot from the wooden boardwalk at the base for a dynamic perspective.

Mirror Lake (Jing Hai)

Why Visit? On windless days, the lake perfectly mirrors the surrounding mountains and sky. Arrive at dawn for foggy, dreamlike shots.

Shuzheng Village

Why Visit? Capture Tibetan culture with traditional houses, prayer flags, and yak pastures. The contrast of vibrant flags against the natural backdrop is striking.

Photography Tips for Jiuzhaigou

  1. Golden Hours Matter: Sunrise (6:00–8:00 AM) and sunset (4:00–6:00 PM) provide soft, warm lighting.
  2. Polarizing Filter: Essential for reducing reflections and intensifying water colors.
  3. Wide-Angle Lens: Ideal for vast landscapes like Long Lake.
  4. Tripod: Useful for long exposures of waterfalls or low-light conditions.
  5. Respect the Environment: Stay on designated paths to preserve the fragile ecosystem.

How to Get to Jiuzhaigou

  • By Air: Fly to Jiuzhai Huanglong Airport (JZH), then take a 1.5-hour shuttle to the park.
  • By Bus: 8–10 hours from Chengdu (Xinnanmen Bus Station).
  • Private Car: More flexible but pricier; the 330-km drive from Chengdu takes ~7 hours.

Where to Stay

  • Inside the Park: Luxurious options like Jiuzhaigou Paradise Resort offer convenience but require park tickets.
  • Outside the Park: Budget-friendly guesthouses in Zhangzha Town, a 10-minute drive from the entrance.

Final Notes

Jiuzhaigou’s allure lies in its untouched beauty. Whether you’re framing the kaleidoscopic lakes or the rugged Tibetan landscapes, every corner promises a postcard-worthy moment. Pack your camera, check the weather, and prepare for a visual feast unlike any other.
